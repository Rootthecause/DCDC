# Build Notes v9-3

Hi there! 
Over the last months I've received countless questions and issues related to building a v9-3 replica. 
In this document I'll try to address them _cleanly_, so you don't have to dig trough the forum discussions, which unfortunately has gotten somewhat convoluted.

_Please note that I'm using YYMMDD, because I'm sick of other date formats. Old topics go last._

|Date | Title | Description |

|260314| Resolved EV 4.5.15 among others issues | An adhesive has been tested suitable to mechanically secure devices against loosening even at temperatures above 300°C with good reworkability. Furthermore, a new isolator design is released to secure the transformer litz to the PCB. Replicas with the isolator part before the 260314 changes can use a additional 3D-Printed part to comply. The HV input connector has been replaced with a Molex 43650-0315 as the previous terminal block did not provide a positive locking. Replicas using the input connector prior to the 260314 changes can use the Würth 691416600002 push-in terminal block, which utilises the same footprint. However, it exposes TS potential when connecting or removing the individual (live) wires. Therefore, another cable-mounted connector is recommended to safely (un)install the converter. All necessary changes have been implemented to all relevant documents and files with the release DCDCv9-3r1.4. Teams who have built their replica prior to this release should re-read the build guide on the pages stated in the revision history (last page).|
|260310| Primary Windings| In previous versions of all documents, it was incorrectly stated that the primary had 28 windings. The actual number used in the v9-3 was 29 windings. If your primary inductance is 153 ± 5 µH, it is likely that there are 28 windings. At 166 ± 5 µH there are likely 29 windings. Using 29 windings results in around 3 % lower currents and around 6 % lower switching speeds compared to 28 windings. However, due to the complexity of calculating HF transformer losses, changes in losses can only be determined with certainty through measurements taken during actual operation. In any case, simulations show that both configurations are suitable, due to their small differences. I would be interested to hear from anyone who has tested the version with 28 windings regarding their efficiency and transformer temperatures - please contact me on Reddit or Discord.|
|260308| Resolving EV 4.5.15| In September 2025, the issue regarding soldering in the high current path was brought up (see issue #10 ). One of the main points was that the high frequency litz could be considered as "wire" and thus not allowed in respect of EV 4.5.15 "the connected devices are not cells or wires". Thanks to a rules question by another team, we received the following response: `"In your case, the transformer should not be considered a soldered wire on a PCB, so it is not in conflict with EV 4.5.15."` Now the only point in EV 4.5.15 remaining open is "the devices are additionally mechanically secured against loosening". The solution has been inplemented in the 260314 update. |
|260220| Replicant induced layout changes | If you are running into issues while testing when using a modified design, please doublecheck all layout changes by comparing it with my original ones (open with KiCad 8.0.9 or compare the gerber files). The v9-3 design was created using KiCad 8.0.9 . I do not recommend for this design using KiCad 9 or later, as some features may be handled differently (e.g. custom rules). In case of issues while testing, please provide your KiCad files anlong the message. Note: I do not accept Altium Files. In this case please provide gerber files.|
|260208|PCBA resistors replaced with milli Ohm instead of mega Ohm| When ordering the PCB with assembled parts (PCBA), the high voltage resistors R1 and R71 in the 2512 package might be not on stock at JLC and therefore repleaced with others with 100 mOhm instead of 100 MOhm. There should be a warning on those components being replaced at the PCBA component selection. Either choose matching resistors (similar Voltage rating, same Resistance and package) or do not place them for PCBA and order at e.g. Mouser instead. A symptom of this is the precharge LED lighting up at initial tests with low voltage at the HV input above around 20V. If this goes unnoticed at higher HV input voltages, breaking U14 might be possible. However, as the precharge is only precharging in this state, the max. input current is limited to 5.5 mA 😄|
|260100|Measuring Lp and Lr correctly|To measure those values, you don't need an expensive Analog discovery. A cheap LCR-Meter works well enough in most cases (I've done so for years before I got the AD2). The donwnside is, that cheap LCR meters might measure based on resonant frequency, which depends on inductance. If it is measured at 350 kHz expect around 1% of increase of inductance from my measurements at 100 kHz. Measurements down to 10 kHz usually do not differ much from those at 100 kHz. If you're measuring Lr, make sure to short only one of the secondary coils. Use a alligator clip to directly short / press both ends of a secondary together - DO NOT use a cable inbetween both ends, as the length of the cable (even a few centimeters) will increase the inductance value significantly! Also keep the cables from the LCR Meter very short and apply open-short compensation.|
